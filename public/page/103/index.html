Interchange, in the context of banking, payments, card processing, and cards, refers to the fee typically paid by a merchant’s bank (acquirer) to a customer’s bank (issuer) for card-based transactions. This fee is a key component in the financial operations of credit and debit card networks. Official Definition and How It Works The interchange fee is a transaction fee set by card networks (like Visa, MasterCard) that the merchant’s bank pays to the cardholder’s bank whenever a customer uses a card for a purchase.
